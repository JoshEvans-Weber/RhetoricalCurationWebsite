3D printing is changing healthcare by giving us new ways to approach it. We're moving away from standardized industrial models and toward personalized, patient-specific solutions that make medical care more accessible to everyone.
However, this great promise faces major challenges. The technology contends with the difficulty of global adoption, the demands of industrial safety standards, and restrictive rules around intellectual property and government regulation.
Clearly, 3D printing's wide therapeutic use and the open-source community that fuels its innovation clash directly with narrow governmental frameworks that currently endanger its necessity
